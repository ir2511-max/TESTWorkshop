AI x Luxury Fashion — Weekly Digest (July 13, 2026)
============================================================

1. LVMH Launches Research Chair With IFM Fashion School to Study AI and Creativity
   LVMH has partnered with France's Institut Français de la Mode to create a research chair dedicated to studying the relationship between science, AI and creative practice in fashion design, backed by a 150,000-euro annual endowment. The initiative, developed with philosophical R&D lab Limn founder Tobias Rees, aims to understand how designers engage with generative AI tools without letting the technology replace human creative judgment.
   Source: WWD
   URL: https://wwd.com/fashion-news/fashion-features/lvmh-ifm-fashion-school-research-chair-science-creativity-1239063072/

2. Bain & Comité Colbert Report: AI Becomes Top Strategic Priority for Luxury Houses
   A new Bain & Company/Comité Colbert study found that 22% of luxury houses now rank AI among their top three corporate priorities for the next three years, up from just 5% in 2024, while 61% place it in their top ten priorities. The report also found luxury consumers are adopting AI faster than brands, with 82% of top-tier spenders using AI tools during their most recent purchase, though only 9% of houses have deployed client-facing AI assistants at scale.
   Source: Zawya
   URL: https://www.zawya.com/en/special-coverage/ai-shaping-digital-future/luxury-brands-turn-to-ai-as-consumers-embrace-technology-says-expert-ymsu7mbc

3. BCG/Altagamma Study Finds 87% of Luxury Consumers Have Adopted Generative AI
   A separate consumer insight report by Boston Consulting Group and Altagamma found that 87% of luxury consumers across clusters and demographics have already adopted generative AI in their daily lives, marking what analysts call the next frontier of innovation for the sector. The study also found roughly 57% of luxury companies remain "AI laggards" with little implementation of the technology, revealing a widening gap between consumer adoption and brand readiness.
   Source: WWD
   URL: https://wwd.com/business-news/business-features/luxury-consumers-prioritize-product-ai-price-value-ratio-1239056055/

4. Bahrain-Based Luxury Retailer Brands Seekers Launches AI Personal Stylist
   Global luxury fashion retailer Brands Seekers has launched an AI-powered "Luxury Fashion Concierge" personal stylist that gives shoppable, personalized recommendations in 38 languages from a catalog of over 350 brands including Balenciaga, Armani and Lacoste. The tool is available in more than 150 countries and lets customers describe an occasion or style need in natural language to receive curated, purchasable outfit suggestions.
   Source: WWD
   URL: https://wwd.com/sourcing-journal/industry-news/brands-seekers-luxury-fashion-artificial-intelligence-1239063495/

5. Generative Engine Optimization Emerges as New Battleground for Luxury Brand Visibility
   Industry analysis tied to the Bain/Comité Colbert study highlights that 70% of AI shopping prompts don't mention a specific brand, meaning AI engines like ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity and Gemini are increasingly choosing which luxury brands consumers discover. Experts warn that "being big, being powerful, being rich is no longer enough," as smaller specialist brands are outperforming larger diversified luxury groups in AI-generated search responses.
   Source: WWD
   URL: https://wwd.com/business-news/technology/luxury-shoppers-adopting-ai-faster-than-brands-1239049435/
